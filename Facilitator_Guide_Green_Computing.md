## Key numbers (so you never fumble a stat)

- **Energy:** ~415 TWh (2024) → ~1,050 TWh (2026 est.) ≈ Japan's whole electricity use. Data-centre demand grew ~17% in 2025; **AI-specific demand grew ~50%** in 2025. Growing ~12%/yr since 2017 (4x faster than overall).
- **Per use (illustrative):** web search ~0.3 Wh vs AI answer ~2.9 Wh (~10x). Images and video are far heavier still.
- **Water:** AI-linked data centres ≈ **1 trillion litres in 2025** (~yearly water of 1.8M people). Training GPT-3 ≈ **700,000 L** on-site cooling water. Cooling = **20–40%** of a centre's energy.
- **Africa:** **223 data centres** across 38 countries (mid-2025) — **<0.02%** of the world's 11,800+. South Africa 56, Kenya 19, Nigeria 17 (= 41%). Market **$3.5B (2024) → $6.8B (2030)**.
- **Kenya:** grid **>60% renewable** (geothermal/solar/wind/hydro). **Naivasha 100 MW** green data centre, **$1B** Microsoft + G42.

## Speaker notes by slide (condensed)

**1 · Title** — Open warm; signal this is a fresh version. Three takeaways: how big AI's footprint is, why it matters here, what they can do. Hopeful, not doom.

**2 · Agenda** — Contrast with past cohorts (efficient code still matters, but AI is the bigger lever). Promise the live calculator.

**3 · The shift** — Slow down. "Every prompt has a price" is the spine — repeat it later. Ask the room who used AI today. The 50% stat: speed matters more than the exact figure.

**4 · How big** — Don't read every number; the Japan comparison lands best. Note estimates vary (that's why 2026-est looks above the IEA 2030 base case) — the agreed point is rapid growth. 12%/yr is the killer fact.

**5 · Personal** — Most relatable slide. ~10x is illustrative; real numbers vary hugely by model/task. The point is the multiplication. Don't shame anyone for using AI.

**6 · Water** — The surprise of the talk. Lead with the human comparison. 700,000 L is *one* training run, cooling only. Green strip answers "why water?" simply: electricity in → heat out → water cools it.

**7 · Bringing it home** — Pivot global → local. Stay balanced; the "honest tension" line matters. Sets up the hopeful turn.

**8 · Where Africa stands** — Spin the small number positively: almost the whole build-out is ahead, so the choices aren't locked in yet. Land: "Africa gets to decide what it builds — and it can choose clean."

**9 · Leapfrog** — The optimistic spine. Use lived experience: M-Pesa, skipping landlines. "Nobody here is waiting for a landline."

**10 · Kenya** — Hero example. Key insight said simply: geothermal is *always-on* clean power — fits a 24/7 data centre, unlike intermittent solar/wind. If asked "is it built?": major planned/under-development project; significance is the model + scale.

**11 · Honest picture** — Name the reality (Nigeria/SA outages, diesel backup). Reframe: a data centre is a huge reliable buyer ("anchor customer") whose guaranteed demand can finance new solar+battery that also serves communities. Done well, the build-out *adds* clean power.

**12 · Sovereignty** — Your credibility slide (audit/data governance at a global bank). Regulators increasingly require sensitive data in-country → local infrastructure → local jobs + control over how green it is.

**13 · What you can do** — Empowerment. Don't read all six; dwell on right-tool-right-size and ask-well-once. Close: "use it like it costs something — because it does."

**14 · Live demo** — Switch to the calculator. (1) Crowd-source a "typical student" profile and type it in. (2) Read out the comparisons (phone charges, bottles). (3) Push AI images/video up to show how fast it climbs. Keep it playful. If tech fails, describe a sample result and move on.

**15 · Careers** — Connect to livelihoods; these are growing job categories, many Africa-based. Optionally mention your own bridge of tech + sustainability + responsibility.

**16 · Close** — Callback: "every prompt has a price — let's build like it." Open the floor genuinely.

## Q&A prep (likely questions)

- **"Isn't AI worth the cost?"** — Often yes. The message is *use it wisely*, not avoid it. Right tool for the task.
- **"Will data centres take our water?"** — A real risk in water-stressed areas — which is exactly why cooling design (closed-loop, air/dry cooling) and *where* you build matter.
- **"Are 'green' data centres real or marketing?"** — Kenya's geothermal advantage is genuine and physical. But yes, some "green" claims deserve scrutiny — that critical eye is exactly what you want from them.
- **"What can one student actually change?"** — Habits scale (slide 13); plus career choices and speaking up. Demand shapes what gets built.
- **"Why does 2026 look higher than the 2030 figure?"** — Different studies/scopes (some include crypto, different boundaries). Treat numbers as ranges; the trend is what's solid.
