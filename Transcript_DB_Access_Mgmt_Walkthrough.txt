[00:00:12] Edwin Joyson: Thanks all. Today's walkthrough covers privileged access management for the database estate. Rajesh, can you start with how privileged access is granted and vaulted?

[00:00:45] Rajesh Menon: Sure. Any privileged database access goes through a ServiceNow request with a multi-step approval — line manager, DBA team lead, then control owner. Once approved, the credential is vaulted in CyberArk and sessions are recorded.

[00:02:28] Rajesh Menon: Honestly, no. The reconciliation is based on CyberArk's discovery scan, which doesn't cover MongoDB. So MongoDB privileged accounts wouldn't show up as unvaulted in our reporting.

[00:03:05] Ananya Aithal: Understood. What about recertification — how often is privileged access recertified?

[00:03:14] Suresh Nair: Application service accounts are recertified quarterly. For DBA personal accounts, I'll be candid — there isn't a consistent periodic recertification in place right now. It's something we know we need to formalise.

[00:03:55] Raj Kumar: And revocation on leavers or contract-end?

[00:04:02] Rajesh Menon: It's manual — the manager raises the revocation request. There's no automated HR-trigger, so timeliness depends on the manager.
